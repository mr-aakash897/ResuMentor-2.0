Optimize for ATS Systems

Most companies use Applicant Tracking Systems to filter resumes. Use standard section headers, avoid graphics or tables, and include keywords from the job description.

  • Use standard fonts like Arial or Calibri
  • Avoid headers and footers
  • Include keywords naturally
  • Save as PDF or .docx format
üéØ Resume

Quantify Your Achievements

Numbers speak louder than words. Use metrics to demonstrate your impact and make your accomplishments tangible to hiring managers.

  • "Increased sales by 25%" vs "Improved sales"
  • "Led team of 8 engineers" vs "Led team"
  • "Reduced costs by $50K" vs "Cut costs"
  • "Served 500+ customers daily" vs "Customer service"

Tailor for Each Application

A generic resume rarely succeeds. Customize your resume for each position by highlighting relevant skills and experiences.

  • Mirror the job description language
  • Prioritize relevant experience
  • Highlight matching skills
  • Adjust your professional summary

Action Verbs That Stand Out

Start bullet points with strong action verbs to create impact and show initiative.

  • Leadership: Spearheaded, Directed, Orchestrated
  • Creation: Developed, Designed, Implemented
  • Achievement: Achieved, Surpassed, Exceeded
  • Analysis: Analyzed, Evaluated, Assessed
üé§ Interview

Master the STAR Method

Structure your behavioral interview answers using the STAR framework for clear, impactful responses.

  • Situation: Set the context
  • Task: Describe your responsibility
  • Action: Explain what you did
  • Result: Share the outcome
üîç Interview

Research the Company

Demonstrate genuine interest by thoroughly researching the company before your interview.

  • Study their mission and values
  • Know their recent news and products
  • Understand their competitors
  • Research your interviewer on LinkedIn
‚ùì Interview

Questions to Ask Interviewers

Asking thoughtful questions shows engagement and helps you evaluate the opportunity.

  • "What does success look like in this role?"
  • "How would you describe the team culture?"
  • "What are the biggest challenges facing the team?"
  • "What opportunities for growth exist?"
üí° Interview

Technical Interview Strategies

For technical interviews, focus on your problem-solving process, not just the answer.

  • Think out loud and explain your reasoning
  • Ask clarifying questions first
  • Start with a brute force approach
  • Optimize and discuss trade-offs
